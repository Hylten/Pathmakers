PHASE 4: THE INTERNAL FIELD GUIDE - HOW A SILENT EXIT ACTUALLY FUNCTIONS

Step One: Build the shadow profile  
Not anonymous. Just untraceable. A distilled view of operational health and strategic potential without revealing the corporate identity.

Step Two: Identify the actual buyers, not the long list  
Most buyers are irrelevant. A curated set of true strategic matches creates a far cleaner signal.

Step Three: Initiate the whisper test  
One-to-one. Not a blast. Not a funnel. Direct inquiry, tailored to each target.

Step Four: Look for behavioral tells  
A real buyer moves quickly, asks precise questions, and aligns incentives.  
A tourist hesitates.  
A time-waster asks for more before committing to anything.

Step Five: Open the micro‑room  
Only when intent is clear. Only with documents engineered for clarity. No bloat. No noise. No distractions.

Step Six: Decide the threshold  
If appetite is strong, the founder can either:  
– Proceed to bilateral negotiation  
– Invite a micro‑pool of two to three aligned buyers  
– Transition into a formal sale with leverage already secured

Silent exits do not eliminate bankers. They place the founder in the correct position of power before a banker ever enters the room.
