PHASE 2: TECHNICAL MECHANICS OF ABL IN HEALTHCARE CARVE-OUTS

Asset Based Lending inside healthcare requires a precise understanding of collateral stratification. Health services differ from industrial or retail ABL because the underlying asset pool contains compliance sensitive cash flows and specialised equipment that must be mapped to reimbursement dynamics.

Key collateral categories in healthcare ABL:

1. Accounts Receivable  
The primary collateral engine. AR in healthcare is diversified across private insurers, government agencies, and patient pay categories. The weighting and eligibility rules within an ABL facility must reflect payer reliability rather than simple invoice age. High grade payer mixes (national insurers, government reimbursement) create superior advance rates.

2. Medical Equipment and Diagnostic Assets  
These assets exhibit stable secondary market values and long operational life cycles. However, valuation must reflect regulatory certification cycles and operating environment. In carve-outs, equipment records are often incomplete. This requires a technical audit before loan sizing. When done correctly, these assets can anchor a robust collateral base.

3. Consumables and Pharmaceutical Inventory  
Inventory eligibility requires precision. Only saleable, compliant, and rotation verified stock can be included. In a carve-out, inventory fragmentation across sites complicates verification. A competent ABL operator maps the physical reconciliation into the transition plan.

4. Contracted Service Pipelines  
While not always eligible collateral, the existence of long term service contracts provides stability to AR cycles. This can influence covenant design and borrowing base formulas.

ABL excels in carve-outs for one reason: the mechanism prioritizes asset behaviour, not corporate structure. During divestiture, the corporate structure is in flux. The assets are not. They continue to generate receivables, operate equipment, and cycle inventory. ABL monetizes this continuity.

Key ABL mechanics that matter during healthcare carve-outs:

Liquidity Engineering  
ABL transforms operational assets into precise liquidity streams. These streams finance separation costs, integration, working capital resets, and early stage optimization.

Balance Sheet Optimization  
Operators can restructure high friction liabilities by rerouting capital through an ABL facility. This lightens early debt pressure and enhances exit readiness.

Asset Hardening  
Carve-out entities often lack clean asset registries. ABL requires forensic asset mapping. The result is a hardened asset base suitable for future M and A due diligence.

Opportunity Velocity  
Transition windows in healthcare are narrow. ABL provides acquisition support without forcing the operator into rigid amortization burdens.

Structural Seniority  
ABL sits senior in the capital stack. This clarity reduces negotiation friction with mezzanine lenders or potential acquirers.
