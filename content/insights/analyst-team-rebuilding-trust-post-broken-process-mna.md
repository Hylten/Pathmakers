## THE REAL REASONS TRUST COLLAPSED Founders often assume trust collapsed because buyers became cautious.

Bankers often assume trust collapsed because debt markets tightened.

Buyers often assume trust collapsed because sellers inflated valuations.

All three are partial truths.

None are sufficient.

The analyst team sees the deeper mechanics: Reason 1: Data exhaust Companies produce more data than ever, but most of it is unstructured, uncontextualized, and misleading during diligence.

Reason 2: Narrative inflation Sellers misrepresent trajectory rather than condition.

Buyers sense the distortion instantly.

Reason 3: Diligence fatigue Both sides underestimate the burden of modern diligence-cyber, HR, environmental, customer churn, supplier risk, compliance, tax nexus.

Reason 4: Intermediary misalignment Banks chase engagements, not closings.

Velocity matters more than precision.

Trust decays where precision disappears.

Reason 5: Over‑theorized buyers Private equity is flooded with young associates optimized for modeling rather than pattern‑recognition.

They validate spreadsheets.

They miss reality.

Reason 6: Under‑prepared sellers Founders underestimate the operational transparency required to justify a valuation.

In short, the infrastructure of trust collapsed because the infrastructure of preparation collapsed.
