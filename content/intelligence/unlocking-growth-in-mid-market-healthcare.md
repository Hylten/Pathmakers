## The Mid-Market Healthcare Constraint: Growth Without a Base

Growth in mid-market healthcare is rarely organic.  

Volume increases are slow.  

Reimbursement schedules adjust at a glacial pace.  

Aging infrastructure offers no scalability.  

IT systems lag years behind payor requirements.  

Talent shortages choke operational execution before growth even enters the conversation.



The central constraint is liquidity.  

Not theoretical liquidity.  

Not goodwill from a local banker.  

Actual deployable capital that can be used decisively.



Traditional lenders avoid mid-market healthcare because the risk profile is messy.  

Revenues fluctuate.  

Regulatory audits can freeze cash flow.  

Collateral is often misunderstood or miscategorized.  

The financial statements rarely tell the full story, and conventional underwriting does not tolerate ambiguity.



This is where private credit and ABL become structural weapons rather than alternative options.



## Why Liquidity Matters More in Healthcare Than in Other Sectors

Healthcare operations experience a unique compression effect.  

Expenses increase faster than revenue.  

Compliance consumes both capital and human bandwidth.  

And the provider must deliver the same level of care regardless of cash flow volatility.



This creates a paradox.  

The business must operate at a high standard even when the balance sheet is deteriorating.



Liquidity resolves this paradox.  

Liquidity buys time.  

Liquidity enables modernization.  

Liquidity lets an operator pursue scale instead of surviving on a narrow margin.



For HNWI and UHNW principals with exposure to healthcare assets, liquidity is not supplemental.  

It is the determining factor between a controllable growth trajectory and a forced sale process.



## Mergers, Carve-Outs, and the Realities of Mid-Market Deals

Mid-market healthcare M&A is not the polished environment portrayed in reports.  

It is an arena of complicated carve-outs, unclear ownership structures, vendor disputes, and deferred maintenance that becomes shockingly visible during diligence.



Carve-outs in healthcare are particularly complex.

Compliance responsibilities are shared.  

A carve-out can reveal hidden liabilities that were never accurately accounted for.



For institutional buyers, these complexities are obstacles.  

For Pathmaker, they are opportunities for structural design.



When a healthcare operator or owner needs liquidity to execute a carve-out or acquisition, Pathmaker evaluates collateral and cash flow differently.
