Precision infrastructure has become one of the most active categories in the mid-market carve-out ecosystem. These platforms sit at the intersection of regulated operations, high compliance friction, capital-intensive equipment, and multi-horizon maintenance cycles. They are mission-critical, but rarely glamorous. They attract buyers who prioritize defensible cash flow over Silicon Valley dream-logic.  

Most importantly, they behave predictably under disciplined capital structures.  

This is the terrain where Asset Based Lending, ABL, creates real advantage.  

And for Pathmaker clients, who operate inside the HNWI and UHNW strata, the combination of private credit access, collateral-oriented structuring, and liquidity engineering changes both the pace and the viability of these spin-offs.  



## Precision Infrastructure: A Market Built for Carve-Out Friction

Precision infrastructure businesses are not software.  

They are operationally heavy. They rely on equipment fleets, service schedules, regulated workflows, and technical labor.  

When these assets sit inside large corporate parents, they often drift into underinvestment. They become trapped inside bureaucratic hierarchies, limited by capex cycles or internal prioritization models that assume every business unit should behave the same.  

Spin-offs happen because the parent wants simplification.  

But investors step into environments where:  

- Revenue quality is strong but understated.  
- Operational discipline varies widely by business unit.  
- Equipment maintenance records are inconsistent.  
- Billing cycles are longer than expected.  
- Vendor relationships are entangled with the parent entity.  

This is why traditional cash flow lending often misprices or outright rejects these transactions.  

Not because the asset is weak.  

But because the underwriting model fails to see what is actually monetizable.
