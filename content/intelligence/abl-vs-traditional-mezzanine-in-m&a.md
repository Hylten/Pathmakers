## The Hard Truth About Traditional Mezzanine  
Mezzanine capital is marketed as flexible.  
In the field it is often the opposite.  

Mezzanine pricing shifts quickly when risk appetite tightens.  
The term sheets look friendly, then the covenants reveal their teeth.  
You get payment-in-kind toggles that artificially inflate leverage.  
You get board observation rights that slow decision making.  
You get a lender that behaves like a minority equity partner without the alignment or skin in the game.  

In mid market M&A, mezzanine lenders also resist niche transactions.  
Healthcare carve outs.  
Asset heavy industrial.  
Cross jurisdictional integrations.  

They prefer stable platforms where underwriting is more about portfolio optics than about operational truth.  

HNWI and UHNW investors know that speed and clarity matter more than optics.  
This is why they move toward ABL before they ever entertain mezzanine.  



## Liquidity Predictability vs Liquidity Theatre  
ABL provides liquidity predictability.  
Mezzanine provides liquidity theater.  

Predictability means:  
- Borrowing against assets with measurable value.  
- Facility usage that adjusts with receivables and inventory cycles.  
- Fewer subjective milestones.  
- Less intervention when the business hits turbulence.  

Theater means:  
- Capital that looks flexible on paper.  
- Lenders who step in when EBITDA misses guidance by even a small delta.  
- Forced renegotiations when macro conditions shift.  
- Hidden fees and compounding features that punish operators.  

Institutional partners know the difference.  
This is why ABL backed private credit continues to increase its share in M&A structures while mezzanine demand declines.

## ABL Enhances Optionality for HNWI and UHNW Buyers  
HNWI and UHNW investors value optionality more than anything.  
Optionality is the ability to restructure quickly.  
To scale without renegotiation.  
To exit without penalty.  

ABL enhances optionality.  

Mezzanine reduces it.  

Here is why:  
ABL is secured against assets.  
Control remains with the investor.  
If the business scales, the facility expands.  
If the investor wants to refinance, they can do so without begging for consent.  
If the capital structure shifts, ABL lenders adjust instead of obstruct.  

Mezzanine lenders rarely behave this way.  
Their economics depend on maintaining a static capital position.  
They prefer long duration and high yield.  
That means they oppose early exits, rapid refinancing, or structural upgrades.
